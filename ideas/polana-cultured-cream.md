I actually like that direction better. One of the themes we've been developing for **Polana** is that it should feel like it *could* have existed in Poland or neighboring countries, just updated for a modern audience. Blending twaróg with Greek yogurt is delicious, but blending it with other Eastern European cultured dairy feels more authentic.

I think there are three versions worth exploring.

## Option 1: Twaróg + Plain Eastern European Yogurt ⭐ (my favorite)

This would probably become my house version.

Ratio:

* 2 parts whipped twaróg
* 1 part plain yogurt

Result:

* bright
* fresh
* mildly tangy
* airy
* almost like a savory whipped ricotta

Nutritionally, assuming low-fat twaróg and plain yogurt:

| Per 100g | Approx. |
| -------- | ------- |
| Protein  | 12–15 g |
| Fat      | 2–4 g   |
| Calories | 90–110  |

That's fantastic. Nearly Greek yogurt protein levels while tasting distinctly Eastern European.

I could imagine seasoning it with:

* dill
* lovage
* garlic
* cracked pepper

or

* chives
* parsley
* horseradish

---

## Option 2: Twaróg + Śmietana

This is probably the most traditional.

Ratio:

* 2 parts twaróg
* 1 part śmietana

Texture:

* unbelievably silky
* luxurious
* almost mascarpone-like

Flavor:

* richer
* buttery
* mellow tang
* incredibly satisfying with hot pierogi

Nutrition:

| Per 100g | Approx.                            |
| -------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Protein  | 11–13 g                            |
| Fat      | 8–12 g (depending on the śmietana) |
| Calories | 140–180                            |

Still considerably higher in protein than plain sour cream because the twaróg is doing most of the work.

This would be my choice for:

* mushroom pierogi
* kielbasa
* roasted potatoes
* braised cabbage

---

## Option 3: Twaróg + Yogurt + Śmietana

Now we're getting into restaurant territory.

Imagine:

* 60% whipped twaróg
* 25% yogurt
* 15% śmietana

The yogurt keeps it bright.

The śmietana gives richness.

The twaróg supplies body and protein.

I'd expect something like:

Protein:

* around 12–14 g

Fat:

* around 4–6 g

Calories:

* roughly 100–120

It would probably whip beautifully.

## I think this fits Polana beautifully

One thing I really appreciate about your restaurant concept is that you're not trying to make "healthy Polish food." You're trying to make **fresh, contemporary Polish food** that naturally happens to be lighter in many cases.

This cultured cream embodies that philosophy. Instead of replacing sour cream with an unrelated ingredient, you're taking three ingredients that have long histories across Eastern Europe and using them in a way that suits modern tastes.

I could even see **Polana Cultured Cream** becoming as recognizable as a house aioli is in many modern bistros. Guests might order it with pierogi, roasted vegetables, rye bread, or grilled meats because it ties the menu together.

One additional ingredient I'd seriously consider is **cultured butter**. Whipping just a teaspoon or two of soft cultured butter into the twaróg mixture (rather than relying solely on śmietana for richness) would add a subtle buttery depth while preserving a high protein content. It would also reinforce the dairy traditions of Poland, where cultured butter has long been a staple. Used sparingly, it could make the cream feel exceptionally luxurious without turning it into a heavy spread. I wouldn't make it the default, but it could be the secret touch that gives your house cultured cream a distinctive signature.

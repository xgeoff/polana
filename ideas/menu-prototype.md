# Polana Menu Prototype

This prototype consolidates the food menu currently shown in `docs/index.html` with the strongest directions from the notes in `/ideas`.

## Culinary Direction

Polana should read as contemporary Polish / Central European bistro cooking rather than heavy nostalgia food. The recurring identity across the notes is the cuisine of the Eastern European garden, forest edge, orchard, and pantry:

- Dill, parsley, sorrel, lovage, radishes, cucumbers, nasturtium, garden greens
- Rye, buckwheat, potatoes, cabbage, beets, mushrooms
- Apples, berries, walnuts, hazelnuts, sunflower seeds
- Farmer's cheese, twarog, smietana, yogurt, cultured butter
- Freshwater fish, pork, kielbasa, duck, broths

The menu should preserve recognizable Polish flavors while making the food lighter, brighter, more seasonal, and more restaurant-ready.

## House Component: Polana Cultured Cream

Polana Cultured Cream should become a recurring house condiment, in the same way a modern bistro might use a house aioli. It ties together pierogi, bread, vegetables, potatoes, smoked meats, soups, and fish while staying rooted in Eastern European dairy rather than replacing sour cream with an unrelated ingredient.

Base concept:

- Whipped twarog for body, protein, and a distinctly Polish dairy flavor
- Plain Eastern European yogurt for brightness
- Smietana for richness when the dish needs more comfort
- Cultured butter for subtle signature depth

Core versions:

**Spring / Garden Cultured Cream**  
2 parts whipped twarog, 1 part plain yogurt; seasoned with dill, lovage, garlic, cracked pepper, or parsley

Use with:
- Herb-cheese pierogi
- Cucumber salad
- Sorrel soup
- Fresh vegetables
- Lighter fish preparations

**Earthy Smietana Cultured Cream**  
2 parts whipped twarog, 1 part smietana; richer, silkier, more comforting

Use with:
- Mushroom pierogi
- Kielbasa
- Roasted potatoes
- Braised cabbage
- Autumn and winter dishes

**House Hybrid Cultured Cream**  
60% whipped twarog, 25% yogurt, 15% smietana; balanced, restaurant-ready, broadly useful

Use with:
- Bread service
- Zurek garnish
- Golabki
- Beet salad
- General service when only one house version is practical

Development note:
- Use a teaspoon or two of softened cultured butter whipped into the base. It should add enough buttery depth to make the cream feel proprietary without making it heavy.

## Pierogi / Dedicated Mini-Category

Pierogi should be a small dedicated category rather than a single starter or a buried main. This gives Polana a clear signature while allowing lighter, seasonal, and richer versions to coexist.

### Flagship: Basil, Dill & Garlic Farmer's Cheese Pierogi

Pan-fried in butter, lightly golden onions, Spring / Garden Cultured Cream, chunky tart apple compote, cucumber-dill salad, buttered rye crumbs  
Current site item evolved from: Pierogi, 12

Execution notes:
- This is the flagship pierogi.
- Drain cottage cheese or use farmer's cheese / twarog for structure.
- Saute garlic gently; add basil late or keep some fresh so the filling stays bright.
- Blend basil with dill so it feels Polish rather than Italian.
- Finish with parsley, dill, cracked black pepper, and a small drizzle of browned butter.
- Use Spring / Garden Cultured Cream instead of plain Greek yogurt or sour cream. It keeps the lighter profile but feels more specifically Polish.

Kielbasa add-in:
- Offer seared kielbasa medallions as an add-in.
- Serve 3-5 medallions alongside the pierogi, not on top.
- Keep onions lightly golden when kielbasa is added so smoke does not compete with deep caramelized sweetness.

### Seasonal Pierogi Candidates

**Forest Pierogi**

**Roasted Beet Pierogi**

**Duck Pierogi**

## Na Słodko / Dessert

### Classic Polana Sernik

Lemon, sour cherry compote  
Current site price: 9

Prototype:

**Classic Polana Sernik**  
Whipped twarog cheesecake, lemon zest, vanilla bean, thin buttery crust, lightly browned top, warm sour cherry compote, lightly sweetened cultured cream, fresh mint or lemon balm

Notes:
- This should replace the generic cheesecake item.
- Use the classic sernik style at launch.
- Sernik is more cheese-forward, tangy, and lighter than American cream-cheese cheesecake.
- Twarog is the defining ingredient. Use Polish twarog if available, house farmer's cheese, or blended dry-curd cottage cheese as a fallback.
- Keep the fruit restrained. The dessert should not be buried under sauce.
- Sour cherry compote replaces blueberry because it reads more distinctly Central/Eastern European.
- Consider a very lightly sweetened dessert version of Polana Cultured Cream on the side rather than whipped cream.

Future variant candidates:
- Sernik Krakowski with pastry lattice and candied orange peel
- Krolewski sernik with chocolate shortcrust and cheese filling

## Resolved Menu Architecture

- Pierogi is a dedicated mini-category.
- The flagship pierogi is Basil, Dill & Garlic Farmer's Cheese Pierogi.
- Sorrel soup should be permanent if practical, with seasonal sourcing adjustments.
- Kielbasa should be both a standalone board item and a defined add-in across compatible dishes.

## Seasonal Logic

### Spring

- Sorrel soup
- Flagship basil, dill, and farmer's cheese pierogi
- Spring / Garden Cultured Cream
- Radishes, cucumber, nasturtium, fresh herbs
- Polana Garden Salad with radish greens and peas

### Summer

- Cucumber-dill salad
- Garden salad with tomatoes, cucumbers, nasturtium flowers
- Pike or trout with dill relish
- Kielbasa board as a shareable item with pickles and rye
- Berry components in dessert

### Autumn

- Wild mushroom paprikash
- Forest pierogi with mushrooms, caramelized onions, farmer's cheese
- Earthy Smietana Cultured Cream
- Kielbasa add-ins for pierogi, potato pancakes, kasza, and cabbage
- Roasted beet and apple salad
- Apple and walnut strudel

### Winter

- Żurek
- Gołąbki
- Pork chop with kasha and braised cabbage
- Duck pierogi with cherry jus
- Kielbasa board and smoked-meat add-ins
- Braised cabbage / bigos-style components

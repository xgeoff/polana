I'd prepare it like this:

* Slice the kielbasa into **½-inch medallions**.
* Brown them in a skillet until they develop a deep caramelized crust.
* Don't overcrowd the pan—you want browning, not steaming.
* Finish with a tiny dab of whole-grain mustard or a mustard cream on the side if desired, rather than pouring sauce over the sausage.

Then place **3–5 medallions** alongside the pierogi, not on top of them. That keeps the presentation elegant and lets diners choose whether to combine flavors.

What I especially like is that your menu now offers two experiences:

* **Vegetarian:** Herb & garlic cottage cheese pierogi with yogurt, apple compote, caramelized onions, and cucumber-dill salad.
* **With kielbasa:** The same dish, enhanced with a few beautifully seared medallions of smoked sausage for extra richness and protein.

One additional refinement: if you're using a smoked kielbasa, I'd make the onions **less deeply caramelized**—cook them until they're soft and just beginning to turn golden. That preserves a little sweetness without competing with the sausage's smoky flavor.

I could easily imagine this being served in a casual upscale restaurant that celebrates Polish flavors while emphasizing fresh ingredients and clean presentation.
